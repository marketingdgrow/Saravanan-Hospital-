FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What conditions does a general physician treat? +

Fevers, infections, diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disorders, vitamin deficiencies, digestive issues, and most day-to-day health concerns. If your condition needs a specialist, your physician will refer you directly.

Do I need an appointment or can I walk in? +

Walk-ins are welcome for general consultations and acute illnesses. For specialist consultations like endocrinology, booking ahead reduces your wait time.

How is diabetes managed at Saravanan Hospital? +

Through regular HbA1c monitoring, personalised medication, diet counselling, and screening for complications affecting the kidneys, eyes, feet, and heart — all tracked consistently over time.

Can high blood pressure be controlled without medication? +

In early or mild cases, lifestyle changes — diet, exercise, reduced salt intake — can make a meaningful difference. Most patients eventually need medication, but the dose and type depend on your readings and overall health.

What is an endocrinologist and when should I see one? +

An endocrinologist specialises in hormonal conditions — thyroid, diabetes, adrenal, pituitary, and reproductive hormones. See one if your symptoms haven't been explained by routine tests or if your current treatment isn't working well enough.
